### Kernthese
The article analyzes how colonial imaginaries were deployed in Danish education during the late 19th and early 20th centuries to foster social cohesion and citizen formation as Denmark transitioned from colonial power to welfare state. It examines how missionaries, civil servants, and intellectuals used colonial figures as curricular content and pedagogical methods, applying Ann Stoler's framework of colonial experience being "put to work" in metropolitan contexts.
