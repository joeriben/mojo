### Kernthese
The article introduces "prompting (l)iteracy" as a sociotechnical capacity that moves beyond mechanistic prompt engineering and human-centered AI-literacy frameworks. It combines iterative prompt revision with critical awareness of economic logics, linguistic hierarchies, and distributed agencies in AI dialogue, using lenses from post-Fordist political economy, Bourdieu's linguistic capital, Latour's actor-network theory, and de Certeau's tactics. The aim is to provide a richer analytic lens for understanding and mitigating AI-mediated educational inequality.
