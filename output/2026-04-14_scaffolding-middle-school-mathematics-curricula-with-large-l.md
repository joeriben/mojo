### Kernthese
The article investigates whether large language models (LLMs) can support middle school math teachers by generating high‑quality curriculum scaffolds (warmup tasks). Through cognitive task analysis with expert teachers, it identifies a three‑stage scaffolding process (observation, strategy formulation, implementation) and tests three LLM prompting approaches. The best approach, providing the model with original curriculum materials and an expert‑informed prompt, generates warmups rated significantly higher than those created by expert teachers in terms of alignment, accessibility, and teacher preference.
