### Kernthese
The article is an artist's statement describing "36.5/A Durational Performance with the Sea," a global series of site-specific, participatory performance events in which the artist stands in water for a full tidal cycle (12-13 hours) in coastal locations threatened by sea level rise. The project, begun in 2013 as a response to Hurricane Sandy, uses film, sound, and ephemera to create immersive video installations. It is framed as a radical call to reconsider human relationships to water and emphasizes learning from Indigenous (Māori) knowledge systems of reciprocity, honoring land/water/ancestors, and resilience.
