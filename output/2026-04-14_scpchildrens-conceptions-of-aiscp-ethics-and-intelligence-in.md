### Kernthese
The article investigates upper elementary students' pre-instructional conceptions of AI in China, focusing on five aspects: AI representation, functionality, working mechanisms, ethical considerations, and intelligence. Using a multimodal approach (drawings, ranking, interviews), the study reveals a wide range of preconceptions and introduces an XY-axis framework to map functionality along engagement–utility and creative/adaptive–repetitive/routine dimensions. Students' views on intelligence range from naturalist (valuing autonomy) to technicist (emphasizing complexity), while ethical attitudes are generally positive, likely shaped by Chinese social and policy discourses.
