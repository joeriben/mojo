### Kernthese
The article analyzes a Finnish regulatory process enabling automated decision-making (ADM) in public administration, identifying four imaginaries of digital public administration: understandable, self-monitoring, adaptive, and responsible. It argues that these imaginaries reflect public authorities' needs to legitimize existing automation practices while depicting technology as unproblematic and neglecting citizen perspectives, potentially transforming the citizen-public power relationship into a one-way street.
