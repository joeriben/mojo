### Kernthese
The article examines how Swedish daily press art criticism dealt with video art as a new art form in the late 1980s/early 1990s. It argues that art criticism was challenged by video art's "identity crisis," and shows that critics used a range of journalistic genres, art-historical/technological references, and focused on formal aspects. It draws parallels to the reception of photography in the 19th century and digital art in the 21st century as similar identity crises of new technological art forms.
