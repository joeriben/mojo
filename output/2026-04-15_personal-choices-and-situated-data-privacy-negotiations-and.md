### Kernthese
The article examines how (potential) users negotiate privacy and make choices about using household Intelligent Personal Assistants (IPAs) like Amazon Echo and Google Home. Using a simplified version of the Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology 2 (UTAUT2) model, the study demonstrates that acceptance of IPAs does not imply access to all household data, shows how users perceive usefulness in relation to device proliferation, and highlights the complexity of data production at household level.
