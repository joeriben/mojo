### Kernthese
The article introduces "algorithmic probing" as a method to systematically test Google's moderation practices by re-running offensive queries across web search, image search, and autocompletion. It finds that Google heavily moderates content related to religion, ethnicities, and sexualities but leaves stereotypes of gendered professions and ageism unmoderated, and that moderation is stronger in English than in Southern European and Balkan languages. The article discusses Google's "hierarchy of concerns" and uneven coverage as a form of algorithmic privileging.
