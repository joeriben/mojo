## Stalked by Our Own Devices: Cyberbullying as a Boundary Crossing Behavior

https://doi.org/10.1007/s42438-020-00210-8

**Verdict:** SCANNEN — The article shares postdigital and sociomaterialist reference frameworks with Jörissen's work, but addresses cyberbullying specifically rather than cultural education, arts, or cultural resilience, thus showing thematic adjacency without substantive argumentative connections.

### Kernthese
The article explores cyberbullying as a postdigital phenomenon using a sociomaterialist sensitivity and Sørensen's (2009) relational patterns of networks, fluids, and regions. Through focus groups and interviews in Saskatchewan, Canada, it analyzes how cyberbullying crosses spatial, temporal, digital, social, and professional boundaries. The central claim is that cyberbullying's strength derives from its fluidity (continuous mutation without loss of identity) and that digital technologies hasten boundary ambiguation, hindering attribution of responsibility in this postdigital context.
