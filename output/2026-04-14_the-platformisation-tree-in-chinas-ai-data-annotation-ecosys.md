### Kernthese
The article analyzes China's AI data annotation ecosystem using Van Dijck's 'platformisation tree' framework to map stakeholders (central government, local authorities, tech capital, Disabled People's Organisations) and their interactions. It examines how these dynamics shape the work-life experiences of disabled annotators, who are positioned at the periphery, experiencing both innovative inclusion and heightened marginalization risks, revealing complex coordination, negotiation, and conflict in regulating the data annotation workforce.

### Methodisch / theoretisch
The study employs a 3-year empirical design with 107 days of participant observation at seven data annotation bases across China and 155 semi-structured interviews with diverse stakeholders. It applies Van Dijck's platformisation tree as an analytical framework to dissect the ecosystem's actor roles and power relations, situating itself within critical platform studies and disability studies.
