### Kernthese
The article analyzes the educational aspirations and future expectations of British Muslim girls and young women in post-9/11 and post-Brexit England. Through qualitative interviews and focus groups, it reveals contradictions in the UK education system: while encouraging high aspirations, it also responsibilizes these students for structural and racialized disadvantages that hinder labor market success, leaving them unprepared to face potential discrimination.
