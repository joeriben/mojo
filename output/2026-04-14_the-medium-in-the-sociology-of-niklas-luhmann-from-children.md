### Kernthese
Christian Morgner reinterprets Niklas Luhmann's theory against the charge of antihumanism, arguing that Luhmann actually centers the child/human being as a "form-giving medium" in education. Drawing on unpublished archival material and Luhmann's paper "The Child as the Medium of Education," Morgner illuminates this novel perspective and suggests its utility for educational theory.
