## Beyond curriculum: Groundwork for a non-instrumental theory of education
_Deborah Osberg, Gert Biesta · Educational Philosophy and Theory · doi:10.1080/00131857.2020.1750362_
https://doi.org/10.1080/00131857.2020.1750362

**Verdict:** SCANNEN — The article critiques instrumental education and proposes an emergent, aesthetic understanding, which thematically touches on education theory but lacks concrete connections to Jörissen's specific arguments about digitality, cultural resilience, or post-digital culture.

### Kernthese
The article argues that education should not be understood instrumentally as serving external curricular purposes, but as an emergent phenomenon with its own aesthetic qualities that generates the purpose it serves. It combines emergence theory with Cassirer's Philosophy of Symbolic Forms, Aristotelian teleology, and the Greek concept of paideia to theorize education's directional impetus as the open-ended coordination of symbolic, individual, and political realms.
