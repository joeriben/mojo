### Kernthese
The article develops the concept of "technopolitics of labor expendability" through a case study of China's Meituan Waimai food delivery platform. It argues that algorithmic management's optimization logic fundamentally depends on the presumed expendability of migrant workers, which is normalized through alignment with China's fetishization of technological superiority and long-standing marginalization of rural migrants. The article challenges universalist portrayals of algorithmic management by highlighting its situatedness in local political economy.
