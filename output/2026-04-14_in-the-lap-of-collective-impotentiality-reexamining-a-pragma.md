### Kernthese
Joris Vlieghe argues that neopragmatic approaches to addressing ecological crises through practices of study require a reconceptualization of thinking from an educational perspective. By reading John Dewey's pragmatic account of thinking through Giorgio Agamben's lens, he shows thinking as necessarily collective, driven by interruption of habitual ways of living, and envisioning alternative futures. This allows for a posthumanist reconceptualization where the foundational subject disappears in risky collective experience.
