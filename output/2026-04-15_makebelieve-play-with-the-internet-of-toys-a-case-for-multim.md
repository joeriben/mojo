### Kernthese
The article examines whether Internet of Toys (IoToys) limit or enable children's make-believe play, combining Piagetian and Vygotskian theories of play as a unity of cognition and social context. Through observational studies of young children interacting with IoToys at home, the authors find that children engage in imitation schemata and create imaginative situations beyond the toys' pre-programmed functions, arguing for a multimodal playscape perspective that integrates virtual and physical play rather than differentiating them.
