### Kernthese
The article examines how contemporary transmedia TV series (exemplified by the Arte production 'About:Kate') constitute dynamic, networked, and material-semiotic spaces of experience. It argues that these cross-media structures are deeply entangled with everyday learning, transformation, and subjective coping processes in a highly mediated and accelerating society.

### Bemerkenswert
- The article employs a media-sociological analysis of a concrete transmedia production ('About:Kate' by Arte, 2013) to conceptualize seriality not just as narrative but as a 'materiosemiotic space of experience.'
- It utilizes a mixture of critical theory (Deleuze/Guattari, Ehrenberg's 'exhausted self') and media cultural studies (Jenkins' convergence culture, Grossberg) to describe how transmedia viewing practices interlock with contemporary subjectivation processes.

### Methodisch / theoretisch
Theoretically, the authors combine Cultural Studies (Grossberg, Jenkins) with poststructuralist theory (Deleuze/Guattari) and sociology of the subject (Ehrenberg). Methodologically, it is a qualitative media analysis drawing on transmedia theory, analyzing the interplay between broadcast content, digital interaction spaces, and audience practices.
