### Kernthese
The article investigates the efficacy of simulation pedagogy for undergraduate transformative learning about refugees through a decolonial lens. It finds that physical simulation engages kinesthetic, affective, and cognitive domains to support initial stages of transformative learning, but also identifies that such pedagogy can simultaneously serve colonial and decolonial purposes.
