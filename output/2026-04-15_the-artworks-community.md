### Kernthese
The article theorizes the inherent political dimension of art as the "artwork's community," distinguishing between Kantian (transcendental aesthetic community) and Heideggerian (actual community) paradigms. It proposes a reinterpretation of the Heideggerian community as a Temporary Autonomous Zone (TAZ) and analyzes participatory art (relational aesthetics) and psytrance dance movement through this lens, arguing that psytrance embodies the Heideggerian TAZ-community while participatory art operates within the Kantian paradigm despite using TAZ as its medium.
