### Kernthese
The article analyzes Gert Biesta's critique of emancipatory education models, examining whether German models of critical education share what Biesta calls the "modern logic of emancipation" (characterized by teacher transmission of objective knowledge and cultivation of student capabilities). The authors conclude that German models present at least a partial alternative but remain based on education as cultivation, which Biesta criticizes, though they offer answers to how students can achieve independence in pedagogical relationships.
