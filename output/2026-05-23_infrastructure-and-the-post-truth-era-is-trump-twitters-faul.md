### Kernthese
The article explores the nexus of social media platforms, specifically Twitter, and the 'post-truth' era by employing infrastructure studies (STS). It argues against technological determinism—asserting that Twitter did not simply 'fault' or cause political phenomena like Trump's rise—while showing how the platform’s technical infrastructure, classifications, and scaling dynamics are deeply implicated in shaping new discursive norms and power inequalities.

### Methodisch / theoretisch

Methodologically, it performs a conceptual/philosophical analysis supplemented by a synthesis of secondary empirical case studies of Twitter interactions and infrastructure engineering.
